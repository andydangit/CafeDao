Banh Mi & Coffee

Banh Mi

Banh Mi is the Vietnamese word for bread. It is also refers to a type of baguette which is often split lengthwise and filled with various savory ingredients as a sandwich and served as a meal. Plain banh mi is also eaten as a staple food typical Vietnamese sandwich is a fusion of meats and vegetables from native Vietnamese cuisine such as pork sausage, coriander leaf, cucumber, pickled carrots, and pickled daikon combined with condiments from French cuisine such as pate, along with chili and mayonnaise.

Vietnamese Coffee

Vientmese coffee is almost always Robusta, which is primarily grown in Vietnam. Robusta is almost twice as strong caffeine wise, with a thick lingering taste and higher acidity. The strong taste, a thicker brew and a few over-roasted beans makes for a different, distinctive taste.Vietnamese coffee is almost always drip coffee. The ubiquitous Vietnamese street coffee stalls utilize rudimentary aluminum drip filters.
